VP of Sales, Salt Lake City + Chicago

Core operating belief: Revenue is designed, not owned. Heroics don't scale. Architecture does. The first 90 days build the foundation everything else runs on.

Four Anchors for the First 90 Days

👂
Listen First
Diagnose before prescribing. Earn the right to lead by understanding the current reality.
🤝
Build Trust Fast
Relationships with reps, peers, and leadership determine how fast you can move when it's time to move.
🔍
Find the Bottleneck
One constraint is limiting revenue right now. Find it. Don't try to fix everything at once.
⚙️
Install the System
The MOR, Weekly Tacticals, and QCRs are the operating rhythm. Get them in place by day 90.
Days 1 – 30

Listen and Learn

Understand the current reality before touching anything.
"You cannot fix what you don't understand. Spend this month becoming the most informed person in the room about your two markets."
People
1:1s with every rep
  • Learn their story, their goals, their frustrations
  • Understand their current pipeline and biggest deals
  • Ask: what's broken, what's working, what do they need
  • Don't make promises. Take notes.
Cross-Functional
Meet every partner you'll depend on
  • SE team, CS, marketing, RevOps, recruiting
  • Understand the handoff model and where it breaks
  • Learn the tools: CRM hygiene, pipeline reviews, reporting
Market
Understand both territories cold
  • SLC vs. Chicago: market size, existing coverage, whitespace
  • Top accounts in each market, deal history, open opps
  • Competitive dynamics in each geography
Verkada
Master the product and the company
  • Full product certification, not just familiarity
  • Learn the ICP: who wins, who doesn't, and why
  • Shadow 5+ customer calls across both markets
  • Read the last 2 quarters of deal reviews and closed-lost data
Day 30 Deliverables
Current-state map: people, pipeline, and performance by market
Initial hypothesis on the core bottleneck in each territory
Relationship map: advocates, neutrals, and skeptics
First leadership sync: observations and open questions
Days 31 – 60

Diagnose and Design

Turn your observations into a clear point of view.
"The bottleneck is usually not what people think it is. Pressure-test your Day 30 hypothesis before committing to a design."
Pipeline
Do a full pipeline scrub
  • Grade every rep's pipeline: real vs. happy ears
  • Identify the 10 deals most worth your time to accelerate
  • Find the conversion breaks: where does pipeline die?
Team Design
Quadrant every rep
  • Map each person: performance vs. culture fit
  • Identify A-players to invest in and protect
  • Identify where coaching is the answer vs. where it isn't
  • Start building the team you want, not just managing the one you have
Operating Model
Design your MOR and Weekly Tacticals
  • Draft the weekly cadence for both markets
  • Define what a good pipeline review looks like
  • Set the scoreboard: which metrics matter, which are noise
Accounts
Get into the field on key deals
  • Join the top 3-5 deals in each market
  • Meet the economic buyers, not just the champions
  • Start building your own executive relationships
Day 60 Deliverables
Team Quadrant map with development plans for each rep
Pipeline health report: quality, coverage, and gaps by market
Draft MOR and Weekly Tactical cadence for both territories
Diagnosis readout to leadership: bottleneck, root cause, and plan
Days 61 – 90

Build and Execute

Install the system. Start compounding results.
"By Day 90, the team should feel a new operating standard. Not a speech about standards. An actual one, running every week."
Operating Rhythm
Launch the MOR in both markets
  • Weekly Tacticals running clean: pipeline, activity, next steps
  • 1:1s structured and consistent, not ad hoc
  • QCR template drafted and scheduled for end of quarter
Coaching
Start the coaching loop
  • At least 2 call reviews per rep per month
  • Feedback is specific, behavioral, and fast
  • A-players get stretch assignments. Laggards get clarity.
Pipeline
Build a real coverage model
  • 3x pipeline coverage minimum in both markets
  • Prospecting motion is consistent, not rep-dependent
  • Top accounts have multi-threaded coverage
Culture
Set the standard by living it
  • The team knows what you expect. No ambiguity.
  • Triple-win is the lens: rep wins, business wins, customer wins
  • Accountability is consistent. Recognition is loud.
Day 90 Deliverables
Full operating system running: MOR, Weekly Tacticals, 1:1s, QCRs
90-day readout to leadership: wins, gaps, and Q2/Q3 focus
Hiring plan if headcount gaps exist in either market
Top 20 target accounts named and actively covered in both markets
Market-Specific Priorities

Salt Lake City Market 1

  • Understand current team size, tenure, and performance history
  • Map the local enterprise landscape: tech, healthcare, logistics, education
  • Identify whether SLC is under-covered, correctly covered, or over-indexed
  • Find the 2-3 lighthouse accounts worth executive attention in Q1
  • Assess SE and CS support alignment in the market

Chicago Market 2

  • Larger, more competitive enterprise market, likely more complex team
  • Map key verticals: financial services, manufacturing, retail, healthcare, education
  • Understand the current competitive pressure from legacy players
  • Identify top 3 accounts where Verkada has a real right to win now
  • Assess whether the Chicago team needs rebuilding or accelerating

How You'll Know It's Working

By Day 30
  • Every rep has had a real 1:1
  • You can name every open deal in both markets
  • Leadership trusts you are listening, not imposing
  • You have a working hypothesis on the bottleneck
By Day 60
  • Team Quadrant is mapped
  • Pipeline quality has improved (scrubbed, graded)
  • Diagnosis is on paper and socialized with leadership
  • Operating cadence is drafted and previewed with the team
By Day 90
  • MOR, Weekly Tacticals, and 1:1s are running consistently
  • Reps know what great looks like — and are being held to it
  • Pipeline coverage is 3x+ in both markets
  • You have a clear Q2/Q3 plan and leadership alignment
